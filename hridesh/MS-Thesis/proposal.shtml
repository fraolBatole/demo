How to write your submission and how it will be graded?

There are two important parts of your project proposal.

  • Related Work (5%)
  • Evaluation Methodology (5%)
and then there are two other parts, that are necessary to make the context of the above clear.
  • Introduction
  • Background
I will explain these in some detail below.

Related Work

An important part of your project is to position your work with respect to the work that has already been done on the topic of your project. Thus, the first step in writing your related work is to search for relevant papers. I suggest that you use the following iterative algorithm to look for related ideas.

  • Init Step: search for the keywords (submitted before) using Google Scholar. Are the search results relevant? If not, you may need to refine your keywords until you find at least one relevant paper.
  • Most cited: once the results of the search seem relevant (by successive refinement of keywords), note the top most papers in the search result. Scholar results are ordered by citation. You should download and read these papers to determine their relevance to your project topic. A quick way to filter out these results would be glance at their title and abstract. Second filter by venue can also be used, e.g. did the paper appear in POPL, PLDI, ECOOP, OOPSLA, ICSE, FSE, TACAS, TOPLAS, TOSEM, TSE, etc?
    • Explore the papers that cite these most cited papers.
    • Continue until fix point
  • Most recent: Scholar results are ordered by citation, thus the papers that appear on the last pages of your search results are either completely irrelevant or recently published (and thus not cited widely). Again, it will be easy to filter out the irrelevant papers by quick glance at title/abstract and by venue of the publication.

Evaluation Methodology

The evaluation of your technical approach is the most important scientific activity. The main purpose of evaluation is to validate your hypothesis about your approach. In writing the evaluation methodology part of your project proposal, I encourage you to emphasize on two main aspects of your experiment.

  • Hypothesis: What would be the main research result? What would be the secondary research results? Phrase these as primary and secondary hypothesis.
  • Experiment Setup: How are you going to setup your experiments that will test these hypothesis? What are the variables in this experiment? How do you plan to control these variables for an unbiased experimental result?
The description of hypothesis and experimental setup are intertwined in the sense that each hypothesis must be tested by a set of experiments.
