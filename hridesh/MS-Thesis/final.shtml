How to write your submission and how it will be graded?

Following are important parts of your final project report.

  • Static Semantics aka. Type System (5%): In this section, you should describe the type attributes for your type systems, give type checking rules for your language design including definitions of all auxiliary functions used. The rules in the static semantics should be described formally as in the form of an inference rule and informally.
  • Proof of Type Soundness (2.5%): In this section, you should prove the soundness of your type system. The proof of soundness of your type system should use the standard preservation and progress argument originally made by Wright and Felleisen and as discussed in the class.
  • Proof of Project-specific Properties (5%): In this section, you should prove that your proposed language design has the property which is helpful towards solving the problem that you set out to solve in your project.
  • Future Work (Extra credit): In this section, you should discuss the problems in the project that remain to be solved. An ideal description would contain an itemized list of tasks that can be undertaken in future to bring your project to a successful conclusions or to further enhance the properties of your language design. It would also include a discussion of challenges that you foresee going forward in your project and reflect on approaches to address these challenges.
  • Addressing Feedback on Interim Project Report (varies): You should have received feedback on your interim project reports. In some cases, the feedback were suggestions for improvements and no points were deducted. In other cases, points were deducted due to the fact that the interim project report did not meet the requirements. Please feel free to address those issues in the final report to get some of those points back. However, please be sure to submit the printed version of your interim project report with instructor's comments along with the final report.
