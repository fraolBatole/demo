Open Positions in the Panini Project

Postdoctoral Fellow

The Department of Computer Science, Iowa State University invites applications for a postdoctoral fellow position. The initial position is for one year with a possibility of extension up to three years. The position requires a Ph.D. in Computer Science, Software Engineering, or a closely related field and outstanding potential for research in computer science. Candidates who are in the final stages of obtaining a Ph.D. will also be considered.

We would like to hire immediately; however, a later start date is possible, depending on the candidate’s timetable.

The position is in the Panini project that is aimed at improving concurrent software engineering by enabling modular reasoning about concurrent software via synergistic advances in linguistic abstractions and verification mechanisms. For more details about the project please refer to: paninij.org and see the following papers.

  • Hridesh Rajan, "Capsule-oriented Programming," ICSE'15: The 37th International Conference on Software Engineering, Florence, Italy, May 2015.
  • Mehdi Bagherzadeh and Hridesh Rajan, "Panini: A Concurrent Programming Model for Solving Pervasive and Oblivious Interference," 14th International Conference on Modularity (Modularity'15), Fort Collins, Colorado, USA, March 2015.

Qualifications

  • Required: a PhD in computer science, software engineering and related discipline.
  • Required: strong background in either software engineering OR programming languages
  • Required: at least one publication in top-tier SE/PL venues.
  • Preferred: strong background in compilers, program analysis, and concurrency.

Iowa State University is classified as a Carnegie Foundation Doctoral/Research University-Extensive, a member of the Association of American Universities (AAU), and ranked by U.S. News and World Report as one of the top public universities in the nation. Over 34,000 students are enrolled, and served by over 6,100 faculty and staff (see www.iastate.edu). Ames, Iowa is a progressive community of 60,000, located approximately 30 minutes north of Des Moines, and recently voted second best most livable small city in the nation (see www.amescvb.com).
